Step 1 – With Spring Or Elastic Band

Place one end over the nodule and secure, stretch spring or band over the opposite nodule and secure.

Step 2 – Now Ready To Use

Check that elastic band or spring is firmly attached.

Step 1 – Attach To Downlight

Carefully ease the downlight from the ceiling about 1mm. Open the Paint Guard using the finger holes provided with one or both hands. Open the Paint Guard enough to cover the downlight. Ensure it remains open and press up to the ceiling around the downlight, and close making sure the lip of the paint guard are between the ceiling and downlight.

Step 3 – Ready For Painting

Your fixture is protected and your ceiling is ready for painting. Lights still work, and the paint guard can be left in situ until all coats are applied.

Safety

As most downlights are on ceilings, step ladders are used. Make sure they are positioned correctly, and if needed, have someone steady them.

Removal

To remove the Paint Guard, simply place your fingers in the holes provided, with one or both hands, pull down slightly open Paint Guard and remove. Make sure you don't scratch the ceiling when removing.

Spraying

To spray ceilings there is a rubber bung which fits neatly into the opening at the top and side access holes. Remove bungs if lights are to be used. Simply insert when spraying and remove, to allow the lights to be used.

Reuse

Carefully clean with warm water and reuse.
